## 1. Seed Validation Analysis

### Results (overnight, reported at session start)

| Architecture | Seed 0 | Seed 1 | Seed 2 | Mean | σ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| EfficientNetV2-S | 0.6724 | 0.6801 | 0.6774 | 0.6766 | 0.0039 |
| ResNet50 | 0.6590 | 0.6255 | 0.6664 | 0.6503 | 0.0218 |

Subset: `ja_natural`, within-site. Seed 0 values from SESSION_2026-04-07_P2.

### Interpretation

- Architecture ranking (EfficientNetV2-S > ResNet50) confirmed at the mean level.
- Gap between means: 0.0263 F1 — modest relative to ResNet50's σ (0.0218).
- **Key asymmetry:** EfficientNetV2-S σ is 5.6× smaller than ResNet50's. The
  primary defensible claim is not just higher performance but greater training
  stability.
- ResNet50 seed 1 (0.6255) nearly overlaps EfficientNetV2-S's floor (0.6724
  is the minimum across all three seeds). Ranking should be hedged accordingly
  in paper language.

### Recommended paper language

> "EfficientNetV2-S achieves higher and more consistent macro-F1 across seeds
> (0.677 ± 0.004 vs. 0.650 ± 0.022), suggesting greater training stability on
> the value-tier classification task, though the mean gap (0.026 F1) is modest
> relative to ResNet50's seed variance."

## 2. Cross-Domain Regression Experiment

### Motivation

Within-site regression-to-tier outperformed direct classification on both
subsets (JA natural: +0.064, BE natural: +0.081). Hypothesis: predicting a
continuous log-price target preserves ordinal structure that 4-class
cross-entropy loses near tier boundaries. The cross-domain runs test whether
this advantage is formulation-deep or a within-site artifact.

### Infrastructure

`train_regression.py` was insufficient — subset support locked to
`[ja_natural, be_natural]`, no cross-domain flag, no tier F1 computation.
Three files written:

| File | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `src/train_regression_cross.py` | Trains on `{source}_cross_train.csv`, evaluates on `{target}_test.csv`. Tier F1 computed by bucketing log-price predictions using target domain's log-price thresholds (midpoints between per-tier means of `{target}_cross_train.csv`) |
| `scripts/run_regression_cross.sh` | Orchestrates all 4 runs sequentially, calls table script on completion |
| `scripts/make_regression_cross_table.py` | Reads 4 `final_metrics.json` + `cross_domain.csv` (EfficientNetV2-S rows), prints and saves comparison table |

**Threshold sourcing note:** Tier boundaries derived from target domain's
cross_train split — not the test set. No leakage.

### Config

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Architecture | EfficientNetV2-S |
| Loss | HuberLoss(delta=0.5), log-price target |
| LR | 3e-4 |
| Batch size | 64 |
| Max epochs | 50 |
| Patience | 10 |
| AMP | Disabled (float32) |
| Backbone | Fully trainable |

### Results

| Direction | Train | Test | Reg Cross F1 | Clf Cross F1 | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| JA→BE natural | ja_natural | be_natural | 0.0873 | 0.0708 | **+0.0165** |
| JA→BE lab | ja_lab | be_lab | 0.1955 | 0.1371 | **+0.0585** |
| BE→JA natural | be_natural | ja_natural | 0.2645 | 0.3054 | **−0.0409** |
| BE→JA lab | be_lab | ja_lab | 0.0768 | 0.1411 | **−0.0644** |

Clf F1 = EfficientNetV2-S macro F1 from `cross_domain.csv`.
Reg F1 = regression→tier bucketing using target-domain log-price thresholds.

### Interpretation

Results split cleanly by direction.

**JA→BE (both positive delta):** Regression generalizes better than
classification when the target domain is noisier. JA images are larger,
brighter, rigidly standardized; BE images are smaller, variable resolution,
darker (Cohen's d=0.77 on R channel). Classification boundaries collapse
entirely on the distributional shift. Regression retains ordinal price
relationships that survive the gap — log-price ordering is more stable than
retailer-specific tier label boundaries.

**BE→JA (both negative delta):** Classification beats regression when the
target domain is cleaner and more standardized than the source. The
classification head partially exploits JA's consistency. The regression head
appears anchored to BE's price distribution and miscalibrated on JA's
pricing structure — BE lab regression F1 (0.0768) is near-random on 4
classes and is the anomalous result of the four runs.

**Primary finding:** Regression's advantage over classification is
direction-dependent. It helps when target is noisier than source; it hurts
when target is cleaner and source/target price distributions are misaligned.
This is a more specific and defensible claim than "regression generalizes
better."

**BE→JA lab anomaly (0.0768):** Likely a price distribution misalignment
between BE lab and JA lab rather than a generalization failure per se. Not
chased with an additional run — flagged as a disclosure/limitation in the
paper.
